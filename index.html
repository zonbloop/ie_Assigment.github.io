Reinsurance

The mixture between Blockchain and Big Data could allow automating risk modeling to know the probability of the amount of money that could be lost in the future, making, as we already deduce, a smart contract that fits with the needs of the customer.

Peer-to-Peer insurance

Blockchain technology can drastically reduce manual interventions and employ smart contracts in order to digitalize procedures that rely heavily on paperwork. Providing an easier-to-access, more comprehensive set of medical, property, or life records on a blockchain could infuse comfort and peace of mind into what, for many, is now an intrusive and often discouraging application process. Blockchain could enhance existing P2P models in order to fund better insurance contracts, and at the same time, decide particular tokens.

Conclusion

Blockchain’s added security and ability to establish trust between entities are two reasons why it can help solve the interoperability problem better than today’s existing technologies, these beneﬁts of establishing an interoperable, comprehensive health record, both in the short and long term, should push stakeholders to explore this technology and may impact how insurance information exchanges operate in the future.
